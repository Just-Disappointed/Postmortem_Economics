Introduction

What do economists mean when they say inflation is a sustained increase in price levels. This is reflective of inflation's inevitability. It is external and ongoing, even in healthy economies. The price level will not stop increasing.
In light of this, healthy inflation is when the price level is growing but at a slow, steady and predictable rate. Such a rate allows salaries and social benefits to (at least theoretically) grow at a comparable rate which stabilizes the standard of living. Like the rotation of the earth, healthy inflation is barely noticeable.
The situation people notice and complain about is when inflation is too high. But what is the threshold at which inflation is too high? While it depends on the country context, there are a couple rules of thumb to consider.

This is easier to assess if the type of monetary policy your government employes is inflation targeting. As an oversimplified explanation, this is when the central bank targets inflation and focuses its resources to keeping it lower. One task that is a component of inflation targeting is the regular publishing of mid to long term interest rates. While Malawi does not conduct inflation targeting, you are welcome to go to the central bank website, reading their most recent monetary policy report and making an assessment for yourself.
