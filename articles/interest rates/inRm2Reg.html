A PROBLEM WITH SIMPLE CORRELATIONS

But wait. You might have some questions.

  • Whether this is a real correlation or a coincidence (are inflation and policy rate just moving in the same direction)
    • “Hey Spreadsheet Woman, so do you also think sunspots make banks fail”
    Correlation can only tell us that there is a linear relationship between two variables. Correlations cannot tell us which article causes the other. It also cannot tell us if the articles actually have a relationship or if its spurious.
  • The direction of the relationship (whether inflation influences the policy rate or the policy rate influences inflation)
    • “Hey Spreadsheet Woman, why are all the correlations positive?”
    A linear regression can help us find the direction of the correlation, which causes the other and the time period. In a simple correlation, this information is muddied.
  • The magnitude of the impact and whether this is positive or negative (even if policy rate influences inflation, does it do so to a degree that even matters?)
    • “I could have told you this and I interned at a bank for six months!”
    Yes, good for you. But a regression can give us a numerical value at the margin to measure the degree that these variables influence each other. This is what makes the social a science.
