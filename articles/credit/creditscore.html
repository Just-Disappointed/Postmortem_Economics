IN (RELUCTANT) DEFENSE OF CREDIT SCORES


INTRODUCTION

I read a lot of 1960s antiestablishment fiction as child and teenager instead of playing outside. During this time, I internalized that credit scores are the enemy. This was not an informed opinion because I am from a country that does not use credit score.

I’d like to use today’s article to revise my opinion.

BASELINE INFORMATION


What's A Credit Score

A credit score is a number banks and lending institutions use to decide how to compensate themselves for the risk of lending you money. Depending on your country or lending institution, this can determine the interest you pay on the loan, the amount (if any) collateral (property you give the bank if you can’t pay your loan) you pay or the maturity rate (Thomas, Edelman and Crook 2002).
